IMPORTANT BL Handling, Storage & Best Practices
⚠ STERILE FILTER BL IMMEDIATELY AFTER DIALYSIS. DO NOT DELAY.
✓ What Actually Matters
  • Sterile filter immediately after dialysis. Unfiltered BL at 4°C grows bacteria whose proteases degrade laccase far faster than any other factor.
  • Weigh for dilution factor, don't eyeball. Eyeballing is ±30-50% error. Weighing gets you within a few percent.
  • Use dialyzed BL within ~2 weeks. At 3-5 weeks you may lose 10-30% activity — a hidden variable between experiments.
  • Dialyze smaller, more frequent batches. ~2-week supply, toss any remainder.
≈ Probably Doesn't Matter Much
  • Fridge light: Studies showing >50% inactivation used direct UV (254 nm). Fridge LEDs for seconds = negligible.
  • 30-60 min at RT in the hood: Laccase is robust. RT is easier to pipette. Only worry at 4+ hours.
  • Ice in the hood: Not necessary for typical sessions.
❄ Stock BL (50% glycerol)
  • Pipette in the hood
  • Thaw on ice, work quickly
  • Parafilm lid before returning to -20°C
  • Avoid repeated freeze-thaw — aliquot if needed
⚡ Dialyzed Sterile BL
  • Filter immediately (0.22 μm)
  • Store at 4°C — do NOT freeze (no cryoprotectant)
  • Use within ~2 weeks
  • RT in the hood for ~1 hr is fine
  • Do NOT add glycerol back — carries over to cells
📚 Recommended Workflow
Dialyze overnight (300-500 μL stock for ~2 wks) → sterile filter next morning → weigh for DF → store 4°C → use within 2 wks → toss leftover.
